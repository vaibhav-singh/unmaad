1. This is a solo-singing event and comprises of two rounds: Preliminary/ elimination (online) and finals (on-campus).
2. Both Western and Eastern music is permitted. Both will be judged separately for the two rounds and will have a winner for each. A participant can choose to register for both western and eastern stream.
3. Participants must register on the website latest by January 20th, 2020.
4. Participant may choose to either perform on a karaoke track or along with accompanists (maximum 2 instruments allowed).
5. A ‘Shruthi box’ may be used by the participant and this will NOT be considered as an accompanying instrument.
6. If the keyboard is chosen as the accompanying instrument, it can only be played in the piano mode.
7. The accompanying instrument will not be taken into consideration for judging purposes.
8. The decision of the judges will be final and binding.
9. The organizers hold the right to change/modify rules on the day of the event. Participants shall be informed in advance for any rule changes.


Preliminary/ elimination (online) round
1. Participants must register on the website latest by January 20th, 2020.
2. As part of the registration process, participant should record a video of a song of his/her choice (recording should not be for less than 1.5 minutes). Record the video of the song of your choice and rename it to “Your Name - Voice of Unmaad” and upload it on a google drive and provide open access to the same. This Google Drive Link needs to be submitted in the Performance Link field while registering for the event (note that registration form includes a field for performance link submission).
3. Please note that this is an elimination round and top 30 contestants (15 for each stream – western and eastern) shall qualify for the finals for on-campus round.
4. Please note that registration for the event does not guarantee participation on the final day. Shortlist for final round will be released and confirmation mails will be sent to the finalists.
5. All general rules relating to the performance also apply to the preliminary round.

Final (on-campus) round
1. In case the finalist opts for Karaoke, he/she should send the track in MP3 format by January 29th, 2020 to ishita.bajpai19@iimb.ac.in ID with the subject “Voice of Unmaad – Finalist Registration Number”.
2. In case the finalist opts for instrumentalists, participants should bring their own instruments and accompanists.
3. On the spot registrations may open on the day of finals in case of less than 30 finalists. Spot registrations will be accepted on a first come first served basis with preference to participants registered online.
4. Finalists are mandatorily required to carry their delegate passes (Participant will receive delegate pass on his/her email once registration is complete) on the day of the final round (on-campus). Either a physical copy or a digital copy of the delegate pass shall suffice.
5. Time limit for the performance is 5 minutes (including setup time).
6. Participants will have to inform the judges regarding their choice of stream (Eastern/ Western) once they are on stage.
7. Performance time is at the discretion of the judges i.e. how long the judges require to take a decision on the performance. One song is to be presented but the judges might ask for more spontaneous songs from the participant with an upper time limit of 5 minutes.
8. All general rules relating to the performance also apply to the final round.

3. As part of the preliminary round, band needs to submit at least two songs. Registration form itself includes fields for the band to submit their performance link(s) (You can put a Google Drive link/ YouTube link/ SoundCloud link/ any other platform link provided it has free open access). Please note that original compositions will be given preference over covers.
4. All general rules relating to the performance also apply to the preliminary round.

Final (on-campus) round
1. Finalists are mandatorily required to carry their delegate passes (POC will receive delegate pass on his/her email once registration is complete) on the day of the final round (on-campus). Either a physical copy or a digital copy of the delegate pass shall suffice.
2. A minimum of 3 participants should be on stage at any point of time and be involved in the song as vocalists or instrumentalists.
3. At least two instruments must be used at any point of time.
4.. Participants will have to perform at least two songs within the allocated time. Please note that original compositions will be given preference over covers.
5. Pre-recorded beats and melodies are not allowed.
6. Equipment provided: A drum kit (you may bring along your own cymbals), microphones, Electrical connections for instruments.
7. In case of any technical failure (concerning mics, power or instruments), judges’ decision shall be binding upon all the participants. In such a case, the participants would be allowed to restart the ongoing piece. Time consumed until the previous song shall be maintained.
8. The shortlist for the final performance will be released prior to the event. On-stage time will be 18 minutes from amp-on to amp-off. This includes soundcheck and setup time. Teams exceeding the time limit will be penalized.
9. Slot picking will be done on the day of the event. The points-of-contact of the bands must be present at the event venue on time for the slot picking. Any band absent at the time of slot picking shall forfeit their chance to perform.
10. Two bands may exchange their slots, provided both bands mutually agree. The organizers must be informed about any slot changes well in advance.
11. All general rules relating to the performance also apply to the final round.
